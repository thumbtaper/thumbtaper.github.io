The Gap

Booking a service is still broken on both sides

Most SMB service businesses run scheduling on notebooks, spreadsheets, and messaging apps. Customers bounce between calls, chat threads, and bank transfers just to confirm one appointment.

Existing tools are either vertical-locked (only salons, only gyms) or generic calendars that don't handle requirements, approvals, payments, and multi-staff scheduling together. Nobody owns the whole booking lifecycle.

For Customers

Discovery, availability, requirements, and payment happen across phone calls, chat threads, paper forms, and manual transfers. No single trusted place to book.

Customers also have no reliable way to stay informed about promotions from the vendors they already trust — deals go unnoticed, loyalty goes unrewarded.

For Vendors

Rentals, coaching studios, clinics, and classes lose bookings to no-shows, double-bookings, and payment friction every week — all because their tools weren't built for bookings.

Generic booking tools carry no identity. Vendors deserve a booking experience that speaks in their voice, wears their brand, and deepens the relationship they've already earned with their customers.

For the Market

No flexible, vertical-agnostic platform handles the entire lifecycle: discovery → requirements → scheduling → payment → confirmation.

Most platforms that come close charge vendors a membership fee just to be listed — putting the cost burden on the businesses before a single booking is made. Ezzy only earns when they do.

Business Model

Simple. Aligned. Commission-based.

Ezzy earns when vendors earn. No upfront fees, no subscriptions for vendors — a percentage of every booking, captured automatically at checkout.
